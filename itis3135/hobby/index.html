What is Fishing?

Fishing, in of itself, is using bait, a line, and a hook to catch fish either for fun or for food. But to me, it's more than just catching fish. It's a way to slow down, reconnect with nature, and let all of my problems disappear. I've been fishing for as long as I can remember, mostly in freshwater lakes and reservoirs chasing largemouth bass and catfish. Whether I'm fly fishing, casting from a pier, or spending the day out on the boat, every cast brings a sense of calm and anticipation that's hard to find anywhere else.

Fishing is meaningful to me because it's a time to let go of life's stress, enjoy the peaceful sounds of the water and my surroundings, and focus on the moment. It's as much about the experience as it is the catch. Whether I'm with my dad, my girlfriend, or just enjoying a quiet day alone, it's where I find myself most relaxed.

I am Christian Natali-Gergich, I started fishing when I was very young with both of my parents, but my dad was the one who first introduced me to it. Some of my earliest memories are of us out on the water together, learning how to cast and waiting patiently for a bite. Over the years, fishing has grown into something that connects me not just to my family, but to the outdoors and the peacefulness it brings.

My mom and dad both fished with me when I was growing up, and now I share that same love for fishing with my girlfriend. When to Go Fishing

Any time is a good time to fish. For me however, spring is my favorite season to fish. The weather is perfect, the vegetation is in full bloom, and everything feels alive again. Spending a day on the water during this time is peaceful and refreshing, making every trip out something to look forward to.

I try to go fishing whenever I have the time, but my favorite is catfishing late in the evening into the night. Waiting and hearing the line run once they pick up your bait is an exilerating experience. Each season brings something different, and that's what keeps it exciting.

Best Places to Fish

Fishing spots can be found just about anywhere, from quiet ponds to massive lakes and open coastlines. Everyone has their favorite place, but part of the fun is exploring new waters and finding hidden gems that others might overlook. Whether you're looking for calm lakes or crashing waves, there's always a good spot waiting.

For me, a few special spots stand out. Badin Lake in North Carolina is where I caught my first catfish and where I continue to catch big ones to this day. Canadarago Lake and a nearby reservoir in upstate New York are close to my heart because they're near my grandma's house, and I grew up fishing there every summer. The beaches along the North and South Carolina coast are special too, because they hold some of my favorite memories. Early mornings, the smell of the ocean, and the anticipation of that first bite.

For anyone looking for places to fish, try searching for nearby lakes or rivers. Even a quick look at Google Maps can reveal plenty of local spots. If you don't drive yet, consider finding a pond or creek within walking or biking distance. Just make sure you're fishing legally and not trespassing on private property.

How to Fish

When it comes to fishing, there's no single right way to do it. A spinning rod is always a great starting point because it's extremely versatile and can be used in almost any situation. Whether you're casting from the bank, a boat, or a pier, a spinning rod lets you do it all. Having a good mix of artificial lures and live bait is key. Worms are a simple and effective choice, and you can even catch your own bait with them. For saltwater fishing, a sabiki rig works well for catching baitfish, and shrimp are great for just about anything in the ocean. Then just wait for a bite, give it a firm tug upwards and you're on!

For bass, my go-to lure is a dropshot when the fish are sluggish or down deep, but a jerkbait works great when they're active. For catfish, I like to use worms or baitfish such as sunfish or shad. The main thing I've learned over the years is not to get stuck on one lure or bait. Try different techniques and setups until you find what works. Patience is just as important. On some days the fish bite non-stop, and other days it's slow. Fishing isn't always about catching a lot. It's about enjoying being on the water, the company, and the experience itself.







Why I Love Fishing

Fishing has always been more than just a hobby to me. It's the peace and quiet when I'm alone, the beauty of the water and my surroundings, and the time spent with the people I care about. It's also the excitement of the fight and the rush when I finally catch one, especially a big one. Every trip is different, and that's what keeps me coming back.

What I love most about fishing is how unpredictable it is. The weather changes, the fish behave differently each season, and no two days are ever the same. There's also something special about being genuinely happy for the people you're fishing with when they hook into one. Early mornings are my favorite. Stepping into the brisk air, watching the sunrise, and hearing the world slowly wake up. It always brings me back to the best memories and reminds me why I love it so much.
